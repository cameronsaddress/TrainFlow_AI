ID: Introduction to Guying and When to Guy
CONTENT: TARGET OUTCOME: Understand the purpose of guying in overhead distribution systems and identify situations where guying is required to maintain structural integrity.

DESCRIPTION: Guying is essential for supporting distribution poles against unbalanced loads from conductors, equipment, wind, or ice. ---
ID: Conductor Preparation
CONTENT: TARGET OUTCOME: Properly prepare conductors prior to connector installation to ensure reliable electrical connections and prevent failures.

DESCRIPTION: Conductor surface preparation is essential to ensure proper contact between conductors and the connector. Surface oxidation and contaminants will greatly interfere with the establishment of a sound electrical connection. An insulating oxide naturally forms on all conductor surfaces exposed to air. Always thoroughly wire brush the conductors (both new conductors and conductors in service) before making connections. Never use the same wire brush to clean both an aluminum and copper conductor. Copper contaminants transferred to the aluminum conductor will cause the aluminum conductor to corrode.
---
ID: Insulation of Connectors
CONTENT: TARGET OUTCOME: Understand when and how to insulate connectors to meet safety and NESC requirements.

DESCRIPTION: Splices and tap connectors on service/secondary conductors up to 600 V shall be covered with snap-on covers (C60). Bare messenger/neutral connectors shall not be covered. Splices on tree wire/spacer cable conductors shall be covered or taped. Always cover any unused, exposed bare conductor. Splices on these conductors shall be taped or covered using gelwrap (C62, C63) or taped per instructions.

ID: Installing Conductors
CONTENT: TARGET OUTCOME: Understand the procedures for sagging, tensioning, and installing primary overhead conductors to ensure compliance with NESC clearances and structural integrity.

DESCRIPTION: Installing primary conductors requires precise sag and tension calculations to meet NESC clearances. Use the ruling span method for uniform tension across spans. It catalogs standard configurations, construction drawings, and application notes tailored to various voltage classes (e.g., 4.16kV to 34.5kV), phases (single, three-phase), and scenarios like tangents, deadends, angles, and joints. This ensures uniform, safe, and reliable installations compliant with NESC and company policies.
